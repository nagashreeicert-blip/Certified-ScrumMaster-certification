1. What is Scrum?

Scrum is an Agile framework used to develop products iteratively through collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement.

2. Who is a Scrum Master?

A Scrum Master is a servant leader who coaches the team, facilitates Scrum events, removes impediments, and ensures Scrum is understood and implemented.

3. What is a Sprint?

A Sprint is a timeboxed iteration (usually 1â€“4 weeks) during which the Scrum Team creates a potentially releasable Increment.

4. What is the Definition of Done (DoD)?

The Definition of Done is a shared understanding of what it means for work to be considered complete.

5. What is a Product Backlog?

A Product Backlog is an ordered list of everything needed in the product, owned and prioritized by the Product Owner.

6. What is a User Story?

As a [user], I want [goal], so that [benefit].

7. What is Daily Scrum?

A 15-minute daily meeting where Developers inspect progress and plan work for the next 24 hours.

8. What is a Scrum Artifact?

An artifact is a key asset that provides transparency in Scrumâ€”Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, and Increment.

9. Who owns the Product Backlog?

The Product Owner owns and prioritizes the Product Backlog.

10. What is a Scrum Team?

A cross-functional, self-managing team consisting of the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Developers working together to deliver value.

# ðŸŸ¡ Intermediate-Level Questions & Answers (10)
1. What is Sprint Planning?

A collaborative meeting where the team decides:

What can be delivered (Sprint Goal)

How the work will be achieved

2. What is Velocity?

Velocity is the amount of work a Scrum Team completes in a Sprint, usually measured in story points.

3. What is a Sprint Review?

An event during which the Scrum Team presents the Increment to stakeholders to gather feedback and adapt the Product Backlog.

4. What is a Sprint Retrospective?

A meeting at the end of each Sprint where the team inspects processes and identifies improvements for the next Sprint.

5. What are Impediments?

Anything that slows down or blocks team progress.
The Scrum Master works to remove or reduce them.

6. Explain Timeboxing.

Timeboxing means setting a fixed maximum duration for an activity to improve focus and efficiency.

7. What is Story Point Estimation?

A relative estimation technique used to measure the effort or complexity of user stories.

8. What is a Burn-down Chart?

A graphical representation showing remaining work versus time during a Sprint.

9. What is a Servant Leader?

A leader who prioritizes the needs of the team, facilitates collaboration, and removes obstacles.

10. What is the difference between Product Backlog and Sprint Backlog?
Product Backlog	Sprint Backlog
Entire list of product needs	Items selected for the current Sprint
Owned by Product Owner	Owned by Developers
Continuously evolving	Fixed during the Sprint
# ðŸ”´ Advanced-Level Questions & Answers (10)
1. What is a Burn-up Chart?

A chart that shows progress by displaying completed work vs. total scope, making scope changes highly visible.

2. Explain Scaling Scrum.

Scaling Scrum means applying Scrum to multiple teams working together using methods such as SAFe, LeSS, or Scrum@Scale.

3. How do you handle conflicts within a Scrum Team?

By using facilitation, active listening, root cause analysis, and conflict-resolution techniques such as interest-based negotiation.

4. What is a Release Plan?

A high-level plan that outlines how product increments will be delivered over multiple Sprints to achieve business goals.

5. How does a Scrum Master support the Product Owner?

Facilitating backlog refinement

Helping with stakeholder communication

Ensuring clear Product Backlog items

Coaching on value-based prioritization

6. What is Empiricism in Scrum?

Empiricism means decisions are based on:

Transparency

Inspection

Adaptation

7. Explain Agile Coaching.

Coaching individuals and teams to adopt Agile values, improve collaboration, and enhance delivery capabilities.

8. What strategies do you use to remove impediments?

Root cause analysis

Prioritizing blockers

Collaboration with stakeholders

Escalation when needed

Process improvements

9. What is Technical Debt?

The cost of rework caused by shortcuts or poor-quality development that must be addressed later.

10. How do you measure Scrum Team performance?

Common metrics include:

Velocity trends

Sprint goal achievement

Cycle time

Defect rate

Team collaboration and morale
(Not individual performance)
